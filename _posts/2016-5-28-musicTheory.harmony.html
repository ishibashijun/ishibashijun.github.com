Studied a little of music theory. These are my notes.

Definition

  • DT is constructed with Major 3rd which is followed by minor 3rd that is followed by minor 3rd. For example, G7 on C Major scale.
  • D' is Seventh Chord that is not DT.

Dissonance

  • Preparation

    • D' must contains a tone which is consonance of the chord before it.

  • Resolution

    • Need to go one up or down diatonically (most likely downward).
    • DT and D' 7th must go downward diatonically which is consonance of the chord after it.
    • IV 7th must become 3rd of the chord after it by go upward diatonically.

Cadence

  • Perfect Cadence

    - V to I.

  • Imperfect Cadence

    - Inverted V to I and V to inverted I.

  • Half Cadence

    - Ends with V.

  • Pragal Cadence

    - IV to I.

  • Interrupted Cadence

    - V to VI and V to III.

  • Faure Cadence

    - IV to V.

Rules

  • Base key and 3rd can not be omitted.
  • Every triads, base key, 3rd and 5th can not be omitted.
  • Every seventh and nineth chord can omit 5th.

Rules of bass

  • Tonic and DT must contains a tone before it(common tone).
  • D' must contains a tone which is consonance of the chord before it.
  • IV must contains more than a consonance tone from one before.
  • D' and DT must go downward 5. In D', next chord must be V.
  • IV must go up 4 diatonically. In next chord, base tone must be I or DT or IV.

Rules of treble

  • If 7th tone is in treble, a tone before must be also a same tone.
  • If bass is DT or D' and 7th tone is in treble, it must go downward diatonically, but if bass is IV and 7th tone is in treble, it must go upward diatonically.

Rules of melody of bass

  • VII must go upward diatonically.
  • F, F#, G, G#, A, A# and B must go downward diatonically.

Rules of single tone melody

  • Avoid going augmented 4.
  • Avoid going Major and minor 7.
  • Avoid going more than one octave.
  • VII must go I.
  • Avoid sequence of jumping progress.

Rules of 2 tones melody

  • Avoid perfect 5 to perfect 5, Major 5 to perfect 5 and minor 5 to perfect 5.
  • Avoid perfect 8 to perfect 8.
  • Avoid perfect 1 to perfect 1.
  • Avoid double VII.
  • Avoid going parallel perfect 5 and 8.
  • In treble and bass, avoid jumping to parallel perfect 5 and 8 with high treble and low bass.

Triads

  • Base, 3rd and 5th of I can be doubled.
  • Let ring a common tone on progress of I to V and I to IV.
  • In the end of a song 5th can be omitted.
  • 3rd of V can not be doubled.
  • Base, 3rd and 5th of IV can be doubled.
  • Base, 3rd and 5th of II can be doubled.
  • Let ring a common tone on progress of II to VI.
  • Base, 3rd and 5th of VI can be doubled.
  • Base, 3rd and 5th of III can be doubled, but avoid doubling 5th without inversion.
  • Basic style and 2nd inversion of VII can double 3rd.

Seventh Chord

  • V7, 3rd must go up one and 7th go down 1 diatonically.
  • In the end of a song, V7, 3rd can go down 3 diatonically.

Nineth Chord

  • V9, 9th must be 8 diatonically above from base tone.
  • V9 aug, 9th must be above 3rd.
  • 9th must go down 1 diatonically to be resolved.

Modulation

  • Phrase Modulation

    - A scale ends with cadense in a section then continue to another.

  • Common-chord Modulation

    - Scales share some chords in a section then a scale changes to another.

  • Enharmonic Modulation

    - Change V7 to augmented 6 or diminished 7 to same chord but different scale.

Nonharmonic Tones

  • Passing Tone

    - A chord's tone go up or down 1 diatonically then on same direction go 1 diatonically.

  • Neighbor Tone

    - A chord's tone go up or down 1 diatonically then go back to same tone with same or another chord.

  • Suspension

    - Letting ring one or more tones of the chord to another chord which becomes nonharmonic then go up or down 1 diatonically to another chord's tone.

  • Appoggiatura

    - Accented nonharmonic tone, go up or down 1 diatonically to becomes harmonic.

  • Escape Tone

    - go up or down 1 diatonically then on opposite direction go 2 diatonically.

  • Anticipation

    - go up or down 1 diatonically to become a tone of the following chord.

Rules of Nonharmonic Tones

  • A resolution tone can not be above an nonharmonic tone.
  • Interval of resolution and nonharmonic tone can not be 1 diatonically.
  • Parallel interval of resolution and nonharmonic tone can not be 8 diatonically.
